alloys

Pure Silver (Pure Ag)

Endowed with highest electrical and thermal conductivity, it has excellent formability. When alloyed with 0.2% Nickel, a fine grain Silver is obtained with characteristics like high wear resistance, good brazibility and weldability.

AgSnO2

The company is one of the few manufacturers in the world having technology to manufacture Silver Tin Oxide by both internal oxidation (melting) & by powder metallurgy (sintered). Contacts are manufactured with or without additives. Special additives keep the contact resistance stable throughout. The standard grades of contact material available are AgSno2 14 %, AgSnO2 12 % and AgSno2 10 %. It is one of the most environment friendly contact material as it does not contain any hazardous substance.

Contacts are available with a backing layer of Silver and optionally, with an additional layer of brazing alloy. Available in the form of wire, rivets, profiles and contact tips.

Silver Nickel (AgNi)

AgNi is manufactured by powder metallurgy (Sintered). Its salient feature includes low mechanical wear due to higher hardness, low contact resistance almost constant throughout the life of contact, low material transfer, low burning, good arc migration and better arc extinguishing properties. It also has better anti welding property. It has good formability with Nickel upto 20 %. If this percent goes higher, formability reduces a bit, but wear resistance increases. Available in the form of wires, rivets, profiles and contact tips, Silver Nickel contacts can be welded directly.

AgZnO

Highly recommended when subjected to intense make and break cycles, this contact material is manufactured by blending Silver and metal oxide powder. It has a uniform distribution of particles and is characterized by a high switching life time in AC relay applications.

With excellent erosion resistance, high welding resistance and free of toxic and carcinogenic content it is widely used for applications like current limiting circuit breaker, earth leakage circuit breaker, general purpose relays, wiring accessories and switchgears(requiring breaking capacity in Ac or DC between 10 and 1000 A and 24 and 600V). It is one of the best alternatives for AgCdo.

AgFe

Manufactured by blending Silver and iron powder, this material is an apt substitute for Silver Nickel. It is ideal for moving contacts in safety devices with nominal currents greater than 32 A and for relays and contactors operating between 0.5 and 20 A. This material is recommended for any switchgear using current between 0.5 and 250A & 24 and 600V.

Silver Iron is used in applications like push buttons, relays, contactors and circuit breakers.

AgCdO

AgCdO is manufactured by internal oxidation (Melting) or by powder metallurgy (sintered). Our specialization includes making extremely ductile wire of AgCdo 15 % which is used to manufacture Bimetal Contacts.

It's advantages are:

  • Good arc extinguishing properties.
  • Low arc erosion.
  • Excellent anti-welding character that offers reliable protection against contact welding at higher current (higher the CdO content better the anti-welding property).
  • Low contact resistance.

AgCu

Manufactured by the melted process, it offers higher mechanical strength, greater burning resistance under most switching conditions and less material migration compared to pure Silver.

AgPd

Harder than fine Silver, this contact material has a higher melting point and exhibits a nobler surface. It is largely resistant to sulphur and abrasion & burning. Available in the form of wire, rivets, profiles and contact tips.
