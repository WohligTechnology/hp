Platinum electrodes have an increasing demand since electrochemical methods of analysis have proved to be more economical, speedy and accurate.

Apart from the electrodes illustrated in this section, there is a wide range of electrodes to suit different purposes. Electrodes of different dimensions can also be manufactured as per the specifications.

Wire gauze cylinders for electrodes are the best as they provide larger surface area than an equal weight of plain or perforated sheet. However, if desired, we can manufacture electrodes having plain or perforated sheet cylinders. Generally these electrodes are manufactured from Platinum/10% Iridium alloy. We undertake re-processing of worn-out electrodes also.

Following types of electrodes are known as “Fischer Electrodes”. These electrodes are most commonly used in laboratories for electrochemical analysis. The stems of cathode and anode are supplied in either round or square wire and can be straight or cranked as shown with or without rings. The helical anode made from round wire is used when no anodic deposition or less than 0.2% lead as lead peroxide is required.

Illustrated here are similar to the heavy Fischer type. The cathode has straight stem and has no rings over it. These electrodes can be used for both stirred and unstirred solutions. When no anodic deposition is required, helix made of round wire is put to use which is comparatively less costly than that of wire gauze anode.

These cathodes too can be used for both stirred and unstirred solutions and can be employed with round wire helical or paddle type electrode.
